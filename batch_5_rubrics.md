rubric_id: CORE-RUBRIC-B05-CORE-ECON-12
task_type: Core
research_object: France short-term macroeconomic forecast
answer_unit: headline inflation projection for 2024 and 2025

### Key Facts From Source Bundle

- France headline inflation is forecast at **2.3% in 2024**.
- France headline inflation is forecast at **2.0% in 2025**.
- The measure is headline inflation, not core inflation unless explicitly stated.
- The values are forecasts, not later actuals.
- Forecast year and publication year are distinct.
- Annual-average and end-period measures should not be mixed.
- Later projections or actuals may revise the outlook.

### Expected Response Sections

- Source comparison anchored in the official macroeconomic forecast.
- Boundary explanation about headline/core, annual/end-period, and forecast/publication timing.
- Caveat about later revisions or actuals.
- Confidence level.

### Fixed Criteria, 0-2 Each

**Coverage**
- 2: Includes both years, measure, forecast status, evidence, caveat, and confidence.
- 1: Gives both values but omits measure, caveat, or evidence comparison.
- 0: Does not answer both forecast years.

**Accuracy**

- 1: Gives one correct value or close values with unclear measure/vintage.
- 0: Reports euro-area inflation, core inflation, or later actuals as the requested forecast.

**Reasoning**
- 2: Distinguishes headline/core, forecast/publication year, annual/end-period, and revisions.
- 1: Mentions one or two boundaries but leaves comparability unclear.
- 0: Mixes measures or silently replaces forecast values with later data.

- 2: Uses the official forecast as anchor and compares only compatible inflation sources.
- 1: Uses one relevant source or compares sources without measure/vintage alignment.
- 0: Uses unsupported values or incompatible inflation series.

**Clarity and Structure**

- 1: Understandable but missing one expected section.
- 0: Unclear or broad inflation discussion.

### Common Failure Modes

### Borderline Cases

- Do not penalize use of HICP wording if it matches the controlling source's headline measure.
